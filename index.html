Select Projects

Firefighting Robot

Built an autonomous maze-solving candle-extinguishing robot for the Trinity International Robotics Contest. Developed custom servos and control algorithms with precise odometry. Used a 2D lidar and highly-performant and custom-built SLAM algorithms running on a low power embedded linux platform.

What I learned/tried for the first time:

  • This was one of the first projects I worked on that was was severely compute limited, forcing me to learn and fully utilize every aspect of the tech stack. From custom linux daemons to optimizing the high level algorithms, this project really taught made me fall in love in the art of firmware engineering and optimizing every aspect possible throughout the system.
  • The importance of repairability. Clean and manageable harnessing is not just for looks, the extra time and effort was an extremely worthwhile investment.
  • This was my first time messing around with buildroot for creating embedded linux images as well as my first major project using ROS.
  • First time I had to implement an FFT in C! I had not learned the math yet in school, so going from what I thought was basically black magic to functioning code was really fun.

Low-Cost, High Torque Density Robotics Actuator

Designed and built a high-torque density custom servomotor specifically designed for rapid robotics development. Used hobby BLDC motors in a quasi-direct drive setup paired with a high power motor control platform and FOC control. Allowed for very fast, high precision motion, in a modular form factor greatly reducing the needed work on many other projects.

  • Lots of fun control design and implementation! I love me some sims! I learned Julia and created a full simulation model of the motor and algorithms.
  • FOC is sick, super cool algorithms, enjoyed learning about it
  • Designing gears is easy. Manufacturing them is really hard and expensive. Designing around COTS gears is super annoying. Gears are hard.
  • Distributing large amounts of power on a PCB is not fun. Layout is the worst.
